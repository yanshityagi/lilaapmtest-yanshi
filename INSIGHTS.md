# INSIGHTS.md

Player behavior reveals a map that unintentionally funnels action into a few predictable hotspots, where most combat happens repeatedly while large areas remain untouched. This creates a loop where players gravitate toward the same zones, encounter choke points, and experience limited strategic variety, further amplified by bots that don’t behave like real players. The opportunity lies in breaking this pattern by redistributing incentives, opening up movement paths, and making engagements feel more dynamic, ultimately leading to a more balanced, exploratory, and engaging gameplay experience.

## Insight 1: Combat is highly concentrated in a few zones

**What caught your eye in the data**  
A significant portion of combat (kills and deaths) is clustered in a small region of the map rather than being evenly distributed.

**Evidence (pattern / stat)**  
- ~25% of total kill events are concentrated in a limited area of the map  
- Heatmap shows a strong red cluster indicating repeated engagements in the same zone  

**Actionable insight (what can be done + metrics impacted)**  
- Redistribute loot across multiple areas to spread player movement  
- Introduce secondary objectives in underutilized regions  
- Adjust terrain to create alternative combat zones  

**Metrics impacted:**  
- Increased map exploration  
- Better engagement distribution  
- Reduced repetitive gameplay loops  

**Why a level designer should care**  
Highly concentrated combat leads to predictable gameplay and reduces strategic diversity. A more balanced distribution improves replayability and player experience.

## Insight 2: Certain areas of the map are underutilized

**What caught your eye in the data**  
Large portions of the map show very low player movement and almost no combat activity.

**Evidence (pattern / stat)**  
- Heatmap shows minimal or no activity in multiple regions  
- Player path density is heavily skewed toward specific zones  

**Actionable insight (what can be done + metrics impacted)**  
- Add high-value loot or objectives in low-traffic areas  
- Improve accessibility (paths, visibility, entry points)  
- Introduce dynamic events to attract players  

**Metrics impacted:**  
- Increased map utilization  
- Improved exploration rate  
- More balanced player distribution  

**Why a level designer should care**  
Unused areas represent wasted design effort and reduce the richness of gameplay. Activating these zones increases depth and strategic options.

---

## Insight 3: Bot behavior differs significantly from human players

**What caught your eye in the data**  
Bots show different movement and combat patterns compared to human players, often clustering in specific regions and contributing less to meaningful engagements.

**Evidence (pattern / stat)**  
- Bots account for a large portion of events but have low kill-to-death effectiveness  
- Bot paths are less dynamic and more predictable than human paths  

**Actionable insight (what can be done + metrics impacted)**  
- Improve bot pathing to better mimic human behavior  
- Distribute bots more evenly across the map  
- Adjust bot difficulty or engagement timing  

**Metrics impacted:**  
- Improved gameplay balance  
- Increased challenge level  
- More realistic combat scenarios  

**Why a level designer should care**  
Bots influence overall gameplay perception. Poor bot behavior can distort engagement patterns and reduce the quality of player experience.

---

## Insight 4: High death density suggests choke points

**What caught your eye in the data**  
Certain areas show a high concentration of player deaths, indicating potential choke points or high-risk zones.

**Evidence (pattern / stat)**  
- ~15% of deaths occur within a small localized region  
- Death heatmap shows strong clustering in narrow map sections  

**Actionable insight (what can be done + metrics impacted)**  
- Add alternate routes to reduce congestion  
- Improve cover and terrain balance  
- Reduce loot density in choke-heavy areas  

**Metrics impacted:**  
- Reduced player frustration  
- Improved retention  
- More balanced combat encounters  

**Why a level designer should care**  
Choke points can create unfair or frustrating gameplay. Addressing them improves fairness and overall player satisfaction.
